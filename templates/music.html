My musical activities

Conducting

I've never formally trained as a conductor but instead try to take/make every opportunity to broaden my experience. I have also benefitted greatly from sitting down with people I respect and chatting over a score.

I have done a fair variety of genres and styles, but seem to find my voice mainly in eighteenth-century repertoire.

I've collated most of my repertoire here.

Opera dei Lumi

I founded Opera dei Lumi in 2014. We did two concert performances of Mozart operas (Così fan tutte and Le nozze di Figaro) and did a one-off performance of Mozart's Requiem in 2016. Realising that for this to really go somewhere (and not just be one-off things that were fun), I began building the organisation from the ground up in 2017. It is now a registered charity with a board of trustees, a clear business plan and – most importantly – an ever growing audience.

Northern Praeclassica

In January 2020 I took over as Music Director of Northern Praeclassica, an a capella community choir in Newcastle. I've worked with them for about a term, but due to covid-19 we've not yet performed together. (We would have done two concerts by now!) I'm looking forward to getting back to it.

Administration

I never set out to work in arts administration, but seemingly it is unavoidable. I currently work as a Creative Development Manager for Samling Institute for Young Artists. As an arts administrator I've done lots of project development, delivery and management, fundraising, as well as more general administration. This has certainly furnished me with a good foundation for managing ODL.
